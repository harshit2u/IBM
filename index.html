Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some of our FAQs. If you have any other questions
you’d like answered please feel free to email us.

  • What is Bookmark?

    A bookmark, in the context of digital technology and the internet, refers to a saved shortcut that directs your browser to a specific webpage. Bookmarks are a convenient way to store and organize the URLs of websites that you visit frequently or that you want to remember for future reference.

  • How can I request a new browser?

    For Personal Use:

    1. Identify Your Needs:

    Determine why you need a new browser. Common reasons might include wanting better performance, enhanced privacy features, or compatibility with certain websites.

    2. Research Browsers:

    Look into different browsers to find one that suits your needs. Popular browsers include:

    • Google Chrome
    • Mozilla Firefox
    • Microsoft Edge
    • Safari
    • Opera

    3. Download and Install:

    • Google Chrome: Visit chrome.google.com and click "Download Chrome."
    • Mozilla Firefox: Visit mozilla.org/firefox and click "Download Firefox."
    • Microsoft Edge: Visit microsoft.com/edge and click "Download Edge."
    • Safari: For Mac users, Safari comes pre-installed. Ensure your macOS is up-to-date to get the latest version.
    • Opera: Visit opera.com and click "Download Opera."

    4. Install the Browser:

    Follow the installation instructions specific to each browser. Typically, this involves downloading an installer file and running it.

    For Work or Organizational Use:

    1. Check Policies:

    Ensure you understand your organization's policies regarding software installation and usage.

    2. Get Approval:

    • IT Department: Contact your IT department or support team to request the installation of a new browser. Explain your reasons and any benefits it may bring to your work.
    • Manager Approval: Sometimes, managerial approval may be required before IT can proceed with the installation.

    3. Provide Details:

    Be ready to provide information about the browser, such as its name, version, and specific features that will benefit your work.

    4. Follow Procedures:

    Your organization may have specific procedures for software requests. Follow these procedures, which might include filling out a request form or sending an official email.

    5. Installation:

    Once approved, IT will handle the installation process according to your organization's security and compatibility standards.
